Steak with Baked Potato and Asparagus

By Christian Ventura

Updated: February 23, 2025

Description

This dish features a perfectly seared steak with a rich, buttery crust, a fluffy baked potato topped with butter and seasonings, and tender-crisp asparagus roasted with garlic and parmesan. It’s a classic and hearty meal, full of bold flavors and simple ingredients that come together beautifully.

Ingredients

    For the Steak

  • 2 ribeye or New York strip steaks (1-inch thick)
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tbsp butter
  • 2 cloves garlic (minced)
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1/2 tsp black pepper
  • 1/2 tsp smoked paprika (optional)
  • 1 sprig fresh rosemary or thyme (optional)

    • For the Baked Potato

  • 2 large russet potatoes
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1/4 tsp black pepper
  • 1/4 tsp garlic powder
  • 2 tbsp butter (for serving)
  • 2 tbsp sour cream (optional)
  • 1/4 cup shredded cheddar cheese (optional)
  • 1 tbsp chopped green onions or chives (optional)

    • For the Asparagus

  • 1 bunch asparagus (trimmed)
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1/4 tsp black pepper
  • 1/2 tsp garlic powder
  • 2 tbsp grated parmesan cheese (optional)

Steps

Step 1: Sear the Beef

Heat oil in a large pot over medium-high heat.
Season the beef with salt and pepper, sear until browned on all sides.
Remove and set aside.
Step 2: Sauté the Vegetables

In the same pot, add onions, garlic, and carrots. Cook for a few minutes until softened.
Step 3: Add Liquids and Seasoning:

Stir in tomato paste and Worcestershire sauce.
Pour in beef broth, add thyme and bay leaf.
Step 4: Simmer the Stew:

Return the beef to the pot.
Cover and simmer on low heat for about 1.5 to 2 hours, stirring occasionally.
Step 5: Add Potatoes and Final Adjust:

Add diced potatoes and cook for another 30 minutes until tender.
If you want a thicker stew, mix 1 tbsp flour with water and stir it in.
Step 6: Serve and Enjoy:

Remove bay leaf, taste, and adjust seasoning if needed.
Serve warm and enjoy!
