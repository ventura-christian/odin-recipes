Steak with Baked Potato and Asparagus

By Christian Ventura

Updated: February 23, 2025

Description

This dish features a perfectly seared steak with a rich, buttery crust, a fluffy baked potato topped with butter and seasonings, and tender-crisp asparagus roasted with garlic and parmesan. It’s a classic and hearty meal, full of bold flavors and simple ingredients that come together beautifully.

Ingredients

    For the Steak

  • 2 ribeye or New York strip steaks (1-inch thick)
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tbsp butter
  • 2 cloves garlic (minced)
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1/2 tsp black pepper
  • 1/2 tsp smoked paprika (optional)
  • 1 sprig fresh rosemary or thyme (optional)

    • For the Baked Potato

  • 2 large russet potatoes
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1/4 tsp black pepper
  • 1/4 tsp garlic powder
  • 2 tbsp butter (for serving)
  • 2 tbsp sour cream (optional)
  • 1/4 cup shredded cheddar cheese (optional)
  • 1 tbsp chopped green onions or chives (optional)

    • For the Asparagus

  • 1 bunch asparagus (trimmed)
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1/4 tsp black pepper
  • 1/2 tsp garlic powder
  • 2 tbsp grated parmesan cheese (optional)

Steps

    Prepare the Baked Potato

  • Step 1: Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C).
  • Step 2: Scrub the potatoes clean and pat them dry.
  • Step 3: Rub the potatoes with olive oil, then season with salt, pepper, and garlic powder.
  • Step 4: Place them directly on the oven rack and bake for 45-50 minutes until soft inside. (Check by piercing with a fork.)

    • Cook the Asparagus

  • Step 1: While the potatoes are baking, line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  • Step 2: Toss the trimmed asparagus with olive oil, salt, pepper, and garlic powder.
  • Step 3: Spread them out in a single layer and roast at 400°F (205°C) for 12-15 minutes until tender-crisp.
  • Step 4: Sprinkle with parmesan cheese during the last 2 minutes of roasting (if using).

    • Cook the Steak

  • Step 1: About 15 minutes before serving, heat a cast-iron skillet or heavy pan over medium-high heat.
  • Step 2: Pat the steaks dry and season both sides with salt, pepper, and smoked paprika.
  • Step 3: Add olive oil to the pan and sear the steaks for 3-4 minutes per side (for medium-rare).
  • Step 4: Add butter, garlic, and rosemary/thyme to the pan, and baste the steak for another 1-2 minutes.
  • Step 5: Remove the steak from the pan, cover loosely with foil, and let it rest for 5-10 minutes before slicing.

    • Serve and Enjoy

  • Slice the baked potatoes open, fluff the inside with a fork, and top with butter, sour cream, cheese, and green onions.
  • Plate the steak alongside the roasted asparagus.
  • Serve hot and enjoy your delicious homemade meal!
